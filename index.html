Stephanie T. Chen

Statistics PhD Student, North Carolina State University

I am currently a Research Scientist/Data Scientist on the Advanced Analytics - US Commercial team at Eli Lilly & Company (LinkedIn profile). In this role, I support the Biomedicines Buisness Unit using advanced analytics, statistical modeling, and optimization.

I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics in 2019 at Department of Statistics. My thesis research focused on developing methodology for functional data applications, with my advisors Luo Xiao and Ana-Maria Staicu.

I graduated from the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor in 2013 with a dual B.S. in Environmental Science and Ecology & Evolutionary Biology. I was part of the Continential Environments Research Group and Smith Lab where I worked on the reconstruction of ancient environments using phytoliths and soil isotopes. My honors thesis was on the nutritional ecology of Golden Snub-Nosed Monkeys, where I spent a year in China on a Fulbright scholarship before starting my Ph.D.

In my free time, I enjoy photography, hiking, and traveling with my dog, Furby, We love to roadtrip and are fond of battlefields and other historical sites. At UM, I was part of the Michigan Ice Carving Team.
