Stephanie T. Chen

Statistics PhD Student, North Carolina State University

I am a 5th year PhD candidate in the Department of Statistics at North Carolina State University. My research focuses on developing methodology for functional data applications, with my advisors Luo Xiao and Ana-Maria Staicu.

I graduated from the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor in 2013 with a dual B.S. in Environmental Science and Ecology & Evolutionary Biology. I was part of the Continential Environments Research Group and Smith Lab where I worked on the reconstruction of ancient environments using phytoliths and soil isotopes. My honors thesis was on the nutritional ecology of Golden Snub-Nosed Monkeys, where I spent a year in China on a Fulbright scholarship before starting my PhD in Statistics in 2014.

In my (limited) free time, I am a volunteer dog walker at the Wake County Animal Center, where I adopted my rescue dog, Furby. We love to roadtrip and are fond of battlefields and other historical sites. At UM, I was part of the Michigan Ice Carving Team.
