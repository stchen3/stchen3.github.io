Stephanie T. Chen

Statistics PhD Student, North Carolina State University

I am a third year PhD student in the Department of Statistics at North Carolina State University. My research focuses on testing variance components for application to functional data analysis. I also work with my advisor, Dr. Luo Xiao, on projects relating to child growth as part of the Healthy Birth, Growth and Development Knowlege Integration (HBGDki) group at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundataion. My research interests are in functional and longitudinal data, with applications to health and environmental issues.

I graduated from the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor in 2013 with a dual B.S. in Environmental Science and Ecology & Evolutionary Biology. I was part of the Continential Environments Research Group (CERG) and worked on projects surveying modern and ancient phytoliths, and soil isotopes for reconstructing ancient environments. My honors thesis was on the nutritional ecology of Golden Snub-Nosed Monkeys, and I spent a year in China on a Fulbright Scholarship continuing that research before starting my PhD in Statistics in 2014.

In my (limited) free time, I am a volunteer dog walker at the Wake County Animal Center, where I adopted my rescue dog, Furby. We love to roadtrip and are fond of battlefields and other historical sites. At UM, I was part of the Michigan Ice Carving Team.
